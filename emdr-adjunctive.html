EMDR & ADJUNCTIVE TREATMENT

WHAT IS EMDR?

EMDR (Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing) therapy targets traumatic stress symptoms. Specifically flashbacks, avoidance of triggers, and intrusive thoughts.

EMDR is an evidence-based treatment that helps process traumatic memories and reduce their emotional impact.

ADJUNCTIVE EMDR

If you are already in an ongoing therapy and want to add EMDR to your process, I offer adjunctive EMDR.

This means you continue working with your primary therapist while meeting with me specifically for EMDR sessions to process trauma.

This collaborative approach allows you to maintain your therapeutic relationship while adding a specialized trauma processing component.

WHO IS THIS FOR?

Adjunctive EMDR is ideal for individuals who:

  • Are currently in therapy and want to add trauma-focused work
  • Experience symptoms of PTSD or trauma
  • Want to maintain their current therapeutic relationship while addressing specific traumatic memories
  • Have discussed EMDR with their current therapist and received support for this approach

I also offer EMDR as part of individual therapy for those not currently working with another therapist. Learn more about therapy.
